Publication: Easter News Round Up WC Apr 11

Transformational change can bring uncertainty and anxiousness to everyone involved:

Setting a strategic direction where the challenges may not yet fully understood.

The articles in this weeks collection look at how projects have taken different approaches to selecting transformation initiatives, empowering teams without overcommitment and the Karpman triangle for resolving conflicts that can impact a project:

The articles cover:
  • Choosing the right initiatives
  • A framework for managing them: PSTIR:
    • Prepare,
    • Socialize,
    • Tailor,
    • Implement,
    • Reinforce
  • Metrics to maintain confidence
  • Managing the work load allocated to team to maintain / grow velocity
  • Resolving Conflict with the Karpman Drama Triangle

Under-commitment leads to higher sprint velocity

Synposis

The Karpman Drama Triangle: Are You a Persecutor, a Rescuer, or a Victim

Source: Making Business Matter : April 5, 2022, 3:24 p.m.

Synposis

Excellent article on how the Karpman model helps explore the relationships within destructive interactions.

This was originally introduced to me on a coaching course.

It proved very useful during coaching to help the coachee understand the perspective of the other people in the situation.

Considering the same situation from allowed the coachee to discover options to improve the situation by moving out of the victim mindset and empathising with the people who often also see themselves as the victims of the situation.

Definite recommended read.
